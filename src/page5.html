The Cosmic Cliffs

Located 7,600 light-years away, the JWST's seventh image captures the Carina Nebula, specifically the edge of a young star-forming region known as WGC 334, also called the Cosmic Cliffs. This area is the edge of a giant gaseous cavity within NGC 3324, with peaks reaching about 7 light-years high. Intense ultraviolet radiation from hot, young stars at the center of the bubble has carved this stunning structure.
