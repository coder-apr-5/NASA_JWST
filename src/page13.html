Pillars of Creation

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope captured the iconic Pillars of Creation, where new stars are born within dense clouds of gas and dust. These towering, three-dimensional structures resemble rock formations but are much more permeable. Dubbed the "Pillars of Creation" due to the countless stars forming within them, we now know they no longer exist, having likely disappeared nearly 6,000 years ago. What we see today is a glimpse into the past.
